About

I am a PhD student in mathematics at UCLA, advised by Zhouli Xu. I'm currently interested in using techniques from equivariant homotopy theory to get at the structure of the non-equivariant homotopy groups of spheres.

Before coming to UCLA, I was an undergraduate at the University of Chicago.

Research

My work lies at the intersection of chromatic homotopy theory and equivariant homotopy theory. I'm currently interested in approximating the homotopy groups of K(n)-local sphere spectra using the homotopy fixed points of Lubin-Tate theories with respect to finite groups. (In slightly less technical language: studying spheres by looking at more complicated and less symmetrical spaces).
